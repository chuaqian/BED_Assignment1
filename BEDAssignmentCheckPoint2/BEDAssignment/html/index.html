Mental Health Basics

What is mental health?

Mental health refers to our state of mind, which includes our emotional, psychological
and social wellbeing. It affects how we think, feel and act, it also affects how we deal
with stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Mental health is important in
every stage of life whether you are a child or an adult. Mental health can change overtime
depending on many different factors. Putting more work/life pressure on someone
then they can handle can negatively impact their mental health. For example if a person
is caring for a very sick relative, or have too much work piled on them, their mental
health can decline.

Is mental health important for overall health?

Yes, but physical health is as important for overall health. If somebody has a mental
health illness such as depression, their risks or getting chronic illness such as
diabetes increases possibly due to the way they cope with the mental illness. Similarly,
if someone has a chronic illness such as diabetes, being constantly worried about it
can also deteriorate their mental health.

How common are mental health illnesses?

Mental illnesses are very common in the world, about one in eight or 970 million
people worldwide are living will a mental illness in 2019, with the most common being
anxiety and depression. In 2019, 301 million people including 58 million children and
teens live with anxiety, with 280 million including 23 million children and teens
living with depression.

What causes mental illnesses?

There is no one specific thing that causes mental illnesses, it is instead caused
by many different factors. Some of them include:

  • Childhood trauma caused by being abused as a child,
    witnessing violence etc
  • Experiencing chronic illnesses such as traumatic brain injury
  • The use of drugs or alcohol
  • Biological factors
  • Feelings of isolation or loneliness
