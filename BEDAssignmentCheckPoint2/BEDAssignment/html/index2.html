Mental Health Illnesses

Illness 1: Depression

Symptoms of depression can seem like its just someone having a bad day, or is
being emotional about something that happened in their life, but its different
During episodes of depression, people experience symptoms such as low self worth,
being unable to find pleasure in things they liked to do, having feeling of
sadness, hopelessness, bring irritable etc. They my also be unable to concentrate
properly on their work, and worst of all, they might have thoughts of self-harm
or suicide. In the world, in 2019, about 280 million people live with depression
including 58 million children and teens. While depression may seem to be a hopeless
condition, they are many effective treatments nowadays, including counselling. And
depending on your age and the severity of your depression, medication might be
considered to help manage your condition.

Anxiety disorder

Characterized by having excessive fear and worries and related behavioral
disturbances. Symptoms can be severe enough to result in significant distress
or a significant impairment in functioning. There are many types of anxiety
disorders, Namely generalized anxiety disorder, characterized by excessive worry,
panic disorder, which is characterized by panic attacks, and separation anxiety
disorder, characterized by having excessive fear about separating from someone
the person as a deep emotional bond to. There are effective treatments for anxiety
disorders, and depending on the age and severity, medicine may be prescribed.

Post traumatic stress disorder(PTSD).

Post traumatic stress disorder may develop after experiencing a traumatic
or horrifying event or series of events. Symptoms include having intrusive
thoughts about the event, having nightmares about the event, avoiding doing
things or meeting with people that may remind them of the event, avoiding
thinking about or remembering about the event, as well as thinking they are
in danger all the time. Symptoms may persist for at least a couple of weeks,
but effective treatment for PTSD does exist.

Eating disorder

Eating disorders, such as anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa involves abnormal
consumption of food or obsession of food, as well as significant concerns over
body shape and weight. The resulting behavior or symptoms may cause significant
distress, damage or risk to health, and impairment in functioning. Anorexia nervosa
most often occurs in teenagers and young adults, and can be fatal. Bulimia nervosa
can result in a significantly increased risk of substance abuse, medical complications
and death. Effective treatments exist, including cognitive therapy and family based
treatments.
